How it works

From raw macro data
to tradeable signals

Every trading day after US market close, the pipeline ingests 10 macro indicators and runs the full inference chain.

01 / 04

Data ingestion

Fed balance sheet, RRPO, TGA, Treasury yields (2y/10y), HY spreads, VIX, DXY, SPX pulled from FRED and Yahoo Finance.

02 / 04

PCA compression

10 correlated features → 4 orthogonal latent factors capturing >85% of variance. Removes noise and collinearity.

03 / 04

HMM regime detection

Gaussian Hidden Markov Model trained on the factor sequence identifies 4 macro states with per-regime probabilities.

04 / 04

Signal delivery

REST API and live dashboard. Includes regime label, confidence, persistence days, expected duration, and net liquidity signals.

The four regimes

A label for every macro state

The HMM identifies four persistent macro states from the latent factor sequence. Each maps to a distinct risk posture.

Regime Equity Exposure Conditions Key Signals
Expansion
100% Fed liquidity ample, credit spreads tight, volatility low. Historically the strongest return environment. High net liq z-score, low VIX, tight HY spreads
Recovery
75% Liquidity re-injecting after stress. Risk appetite healing but not fully restored. Positive but cautious. Rising net liq, normalizing spreads, VIX declining
Tightening
25% Fed hiking or draining liquidity. Credit spreads widening. Defensive positioning, avoid duration risk. Declining net liq, rising 2y yield, widening HY
Risk-Off
0% Crisis or acute stress. Emergency liquidity injections, spiking spreads and vol. Capital preservation first. VIX spike, credit stress, RRPO drain or surge
