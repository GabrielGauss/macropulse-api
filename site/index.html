Methodology

The inference chain

Every trading day after US market close, the pipeline runs automatically. No manual intervention. No editorial discretion.

Step 1 · Ingest

Raw data pull

Fed balance sheet, RRPO, TGA, 2y/10y yields, HY spreads, VIX, DXY, SPX — sourced from FRED and Yahoo Finance.

FRED API · yfinance
10 macro series · daily
Step 2 · Compress

PCA dimensionality

Principal Component Analysis reduces 10 correlated inputs to 4 orthogonal latent factors, capturing >85% of variance.

PCA · 4 components
>85% variance explained
Step 3 · Classify

HMM Viterbi decode

A Gaussian Hidden Markov Model runs Viterbi decoding on the factor sequence to assign a regime label and per-state probability vector.

Gaussian HMM · 4 states
Viterbi algorithm
Step 4 · Deliver

REST API response

Regime label, confidence, persistence days, expected duration, net liquidity z-score, and PCA factor scores — at a single endpoint.

REST + WebSocket
JSON · updated 18:30 UTC
The four regimes

Every macro state has a label

The HMM identifies four persistent states from the latent factor sequence. Each maps to a distinct risk posture and expected asset behaviour.

Expansion 100% EQ

Fed liquidity ample, credit spreads tight, volatility suppressed. The highest-return macro environment across asset classes.

net_liq_zscore > +1 · VIX < 15 · HY spreads tight
Recovery 75% EQ

Liquidity re-injecting after stress. Risk appetite healing but not fully restored — positive bias with elevated caution.

net_liq rising · spreads normalising · VIX declining
Tightening 25% EQ

Fed hiking or draining liquidity. Credit spreads widening. Defensive positioning — avoid duration risk and leveraged exposure.

net_liq falling · 2y yield rising · HY spreads widening
Risk-Off 0% EQ

Acute stress or crisis. Emergency liquidity injections, spiking spreads and volatility. Capital preservation is the only objective.

VIX spike · credit stress · RRPO drain or surge
